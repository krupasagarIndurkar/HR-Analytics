1. Employee Attrition
Some departments show higher attrition
Employees with lower experience tend to leave more

2. Department-wise Analysis
Few departments have more employees + more exits
Some departments are stable with low attrition

3. Salary Impact
Employees with lower salary range leave more
High salary employees are more stable

4. Age Group Analysis
Young employees (20–30) show higher attrition
Mid-age employees are more stable

5. Job Role Insights
Some roles have very high turnover
Critical roles may need retention strategies

6. Experience / Tenure
Employees with less years in company leave more
Long-term employees are loyal

7. Work-Life Balance
Poor work-life balance correlates with attrition
Employees with better balance stay longer


👉 Insight: 

1. Losing good talent → need recognition & growth opportunities
2. Attrition is higher among early-stage employees → need better onboarding & engagement
3. Problem is not everywhere → focus on specific departments
4. Compensation plays a key role in retention
5. Younger workforce switches jobs more frequently
6. Role-specific issues exist → not a general problem
7. First 1–2 years are crucial for retention
8. Employee satisfaction directly impacts retention
